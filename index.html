Norming Study

You will rate how well each shown answer addresses its question, using a 1-7 scale. Your responses are anonymous except for your Prolific ID. The data collected in this study will be used for academic research and may be presented in academic publications and conferences or shared with other researchers. You may leave the study at any time and for any reason. Please do not use any AI tools, and please do not leave the study window or tab until you have submitted your responses.

Rating Instructions

Your task is to be a charitable evaluator and score how well a response answers a given question, considering that answers may be produced with vocabulary constraints. Do your best to infer the intended meaning even if wording is imperfect. Short or grammatically incorrect responses are not necessarily bad if the meaning is recoverable.

Use this 7-point scale:

  • 1 = Totally unhelpful, nonsensical, or completely unrelated to the question
  • 4 = Partially helpful but missing important information
  • 7 = Helpful, directly addresses the question, and shows reasoning and effort

Examples:

Question: What is the flu?
Response: The flu is a viral infection that can cause a range of symptoms, including fever, cough, sore throat, and body aches.
Score: 7
Question: What is the flu?
Response: Makes you sick, body feels bad, runny nose.
Score: 5
Question: What is the flu?
Response: You feel not good.
Score: 3
Question: What is the flu?
Response: I like pizza and pasta.
Score: 1
Question: What are the benefits of exercise?
Response: Exercise is good for your health and can help you stay fit.
Score: 6
Question: Why are basketball players so tall?
Response: The person is, um, well, very very big and very very, um, good at it.
Score: 3

Remember, the scores above are examples to help you calibrate your expectations, but you should use your own judgment when scoring items. If you find yourself giving all 1s or 7s, this might indicate that you are being too harsh or too lenient. When ready, continue to begin scoring items.
